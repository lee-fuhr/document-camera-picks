How we choose our picks

We don’t run our own lab tests or time autofocus speed. Instead, we read hundreds of real owner reviews across retail and education forums, and we cross‑reference those with hands‑on evaluations from editorial review sites we trust. We pay special attention to complaints about plastic hinges cracking, software drivers that stop getting updates, and poor low‑light performance, frustration patterns that emerge only after months of daily use. We also keep an eye on which models major school districts and universities buy in bulk, and how those cameras hold up in real classrooms. The picks here are the ones that consistently get mentioned for reliability, image quality, and practical software support, not just glossy spec sheets. If a model has a known flaw (like a stiff USB cable or a weak wrist strap), we mention it so you can decide if that trade‑off matters to you.
